Sajta

Sajta is a dish typical of western Bolivia, particularly the city of La Paz. It is usually eaten for All Saints' Day and during Carnival. It is accompanied with rice, potato, tunta (freeze-dried potato), and sarsa (onion and tomato garnish).

Ingredients

  • 1 large chicken
  • 4 large onions
  • 1 tomato
  • 250 gr peas
  • Minced quirquina or parsley
  • Chopped celery
  • Salt, oil
  • 6 yellow chili peppers (Bolivian aj√≠ amarillo), seeded
  • 2 tablespoons black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 4 garlic cloves

Instructions

  1. Wash and cut chicken into pieces
  2. Chop three onions and fry in oil. Add peas, parsley, garlic, salt and stir a few minutes.
  3. Add black pepper, cumin and chili peppers.
  4. Add chicken; add sufficient water to cover and continue to boil.
